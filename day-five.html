Day five

This is my last day of the trip. The last thing I wanted to visit was the Bristol Museum & Art Gallery, and I spent almost the entire day in there, admiring anthropologies, historical objects and numerous art pieces.

Bristol Museum & Art Gallery

The Bristol Museum and Art Gallery sit upon a hill on Park Street, near the University of Bristol. The walk up was indeed hilly but it's such a different part of town I have not been to as much. The Edwardian Baroque architecture of the museum was so beautiful, both exterior and interior, and it was much larger inside than it seemed.

The museum and gallery offers a wide range of exhibitions inside, from ancient Egypt history to wildlife, minerals, geology, European art, Victorian art, modern and contemporary art, paintings, sculptures, even pottery, silverware and glassware. (Bristol Museums, n.d.) Its free admission welcomes everyone, with a cafe and a museum shop inside.

Park Street

After several hours inside the museum, I walked along Park street and explore some more shops, it's a less touristy area, with more local arts and crafts, bookstores, boutiques, bars and restaurants. (Visit Bristol)

This concludes my 5-day trip in Bristol, I had a great time and fell in love with the city, I'm looking forward to returning and exploring more in the future :-)
