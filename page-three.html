sightseeing & shopping
Day three

I used a lot of energy on the second day. So I decided to do something more touristy and hopped on a sightseeing bus around the city, then visited the shopping quarter.

TOOT Bus

The TOOT bus is a hop-on hop-off sightseeing bus service, where customers buy a one-time ticket and take the bus as much as they want over 2 days. The Bristol tour route has 14 stops, taking tourists around the city centre area and past the city's top landmarks and attractions, such as the Clifton suspension Bridge and Observatory, Bristol Zoo Gardens, Bristol Aquarium, and many more. The buses also have an audio guide commentary service on board. (TOOT Bus Bristol)

The bus was an easy way to explore the city out of the central harbour area, and I got to see famous attractions like the clifton suspension bridge.

Clifton Suspension Bridge

The Clifton suspension bridge is one of Bristol's most famous and recognisable sightseeing attractions. It "marks a turning point in the history of engineering and has come to symbolise a city of original thinkers and independent spirit." (Clifton Suspension Bridge Trust). There is a visitor centre and guided tours, events and activities are available. Although I was only able to see it from the bus view, it would be nice to get another view of the bridge more up close.

I then hopped off the bus to have lunch at Castle Park, a green space overlooking river Avon. Its location close to the Bristol Bridge, Old City and the shopping quarter makes it a popular park in the city centre.

Shopping Quarter - Cabot Circus mall

After that, I walked over to the shopping quarter, where there are lots of retail stores, cafes, restaurants and malls across several streets, including Cabot Circus shopping centre. Did some shopping then stopped for a meal at Wagamama.

The rest of the day was spent in the shopping quarter, and I ended the day with some mango gelato.
