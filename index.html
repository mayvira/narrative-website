Location Where even is Bristol?

Bristol is the most populous city in Southwest England, to the West of London. Its location on the River Avon made it a major port for voyages and trades around Europe. Since then, Bristol has transformed into a cultural and heritage hub, whilst also attracting tourists and students from all over the globe.


View Larger Map

Why Bristol? What's there to see?

Bristol is home to many famous attractions, such as the Clifton suspension bridge, Clifton observatory, and the Brunel SS Great Britain Ship. It has been named "The best city to live in Britain" several times due to its bustling economy, culture, variety in ethnic backgrounds, and close proximity to nature.

Travel How do I get there?

A train to Bristol Temple Meads Station will get you right to the city centre. Bristol is relatively easy to travel to from big cities like London or Manchester, approximately 1.40 hours from London and 3 hours from Manchester.

Transport How do I get around?

For me, Bristol is a very walkable city, although relatively hilly, it was fun to explore the city on foot. The most popular method of transport for both locals and visitors is Voi Electronic Scooters, they are scattered around the city and are easy to use and pay for. Bicycles are the second popular.
