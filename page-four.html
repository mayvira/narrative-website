chill day
Day four

The forth day of my trip fell on a bank holiday, so most places were closed and I did not plan much. Spent the day reading in a park and strolling around the central harbour area. I had a great time enjoying my solitude.

Queen Square

Queen Square is a Georgian park in the centre of Bristol, close to the harbourside. It is a popular retreat spot for nearby residents and visitors who want to relax and spend time in greenery, whilst still being close to the city centre.

(https://visitbristol.co.uk/things-to-do/queen-square-p38651)

I spent most of that morning reading in the park, it was very peaceful and relaxing.

After a couple hours of reading, I walked to the harbourside and old city area to explore some more, went into a couple shops like a vintage clothes shop, and stopped to have some hot chocolate at a cafe.

Street art & Graffiti

I walked around the central harbourside some more and found some really interesting graffitis. Bristol is home to many graffiti works, and known as "one of the best places in Europe for street art."

(https://theculturetrip.com/europe/united-kingdom/england/articles/graffiti-galore-the-best-street-art-in-bristol/)

I did not take much more photos for the rest of the day, but I wandered around the city centre, appreciating the harbourside view.
