Day four

The forth day of my trip fell on a bank holiday, so most places were closed and I did not plan much. Spent the day reading in a park and strolling around the central harbour area. I had a great time enjoying my solitude.

Queen Square

Queen Square is a Georgian park area in the centre of Bristol, close to the harbourside. The square is surrounded by Georgian town houses on all sides, making the area feel like a retreat from all the buildings and houses in the area. It is a popular retreat spot for nearby residents and visitors who want to relax and spend time in greenery, whilst still being close to the city centre.

I spent most of that morning reading in the park, it was very peaceful and relaxing. https://visitbristol.co.uk/things-to-do/queen-square-p38651

After a couple hours of reading, I walked to the harbourside and old city area to explore some more, went into a couple shops like a vintage clothes shop, and stopped to have some hot chocolate at a cafe.

Street art & Graffiti

I walked around the central harbourside some more and found some really interesting graffitis. Bristol is known as the home to a wide range of famous street art and graffiti pieces, such as Banksy's works. Bristol is said to be "one of the best places in Europe for street art." (https://theculturetrip.com/europe/united-kingdom/england/articles/graffiti-galore-the-best-street-art-in-bristol/)

I did not take much more photos for the rest of the day, but I wandered around the city centre
