museum & gallery
Day five

This is my last day of the trip. The last thing I wanted to visit was the Bristol Museum & Art Gallery, and I spent almost the entire day in there, admiring anthropologies, historical objects and numerous art pieces.

Bristol Museum & Art Gallery

The Bristol Museum and Art Gallery

The museum and gallery was much larger than it seemed from the outside, with a wide range of exhibitions inside, from ancient Egypt history to wildlife, geology, paintings and even pottery and glassware. Below are some of the pieces I found interesting.

Clifton Suspension Bridge

The Clifton suspension bridge is one of Bristol's most famous and recognisable sightseeing attractions. It "marks a turning point in the history of engineering and has come to symbolise a city of original thinkers and independent spirit." (https://cliftonbridge.org.uk/). There is a visitor centre and guided tours, events and activities are available. Although I was only able to see it from the bus view, it would be nice to get another view of the bridge more up close.

I then hopped off the bus to have lunch at Castle Park, a green space overlooking river Avon. Its location close to the Bristol Bridge, Old City and the shopping quarter makes it a popular park in the city centre.

Shopping Quarter - Cabot Circus mall

After that, I walked over to the shopping quarter, where there are lots of retail stores, cafes, restaurants and malls across several streets, including Cabot Circus shopping centre. Did some shopping then stopped for a meal at Wagamama.

Park Street

After the museum, I walked along Park street and explore some more shops

This concludes my 5-day trip in Bristol, overall I had a great time and fell in love with the city, I'm definitely looking forward to returning and explore more of the city in the future :-)
