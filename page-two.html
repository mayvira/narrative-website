markets & arts
Day two

The second day is the main eventful day of this trip, I went to St Nicholas Market in Old City Area, then went to the Van Gogh Immersive Experience pop-up exhibition that was being held in Bristol at the time. It's definitely one of my favourite days from this trip.

St Nicholas Markets

St Nicholas Markets is a market area of indoor stalls, street food, local shops and historic architecture, in the heart of Bristol old city, it was established in 1743. (https://www.bristol.gov.uk/st-nicholas-markets)

Local shops & stalls

There were lots of local shops and stalls in the market area, from jewelry to musical instruments shop, an artist's studio space and a bookshop. I stumbled upon this beautiful little secondhand bookshop and even got myself a book!

I then walked through the street food market and the indoor market area, out to the other side of the building where there were several other local shops, cafes and restaurants, including an outdoor vintage print stall. It was a Saturday and sunny out so the street was bustling with people, both locals and tourists.

Van Gogh Immersive Experience

After that, I went to the Van Gogh pop up exhibition which was being held in Propyard, an event venue in the city centre. The immersive experience involves image projection, sound and exhibitions around the life and works of Vincent Van Gogh.

I really enjoyed the exhibition. The overall atmosphere of seeing Van Gogh's work in such a large scale, together with the music score created such a peaceful environment, I think I spent more than 2 hours in the main room, just taking everything in. There was also a vr experience and coloring activities, which I just couldn't miss.

After what felt like forever in the exhibition, I came out and walked back into the city centre area, where I spent the remaining of the day walking aroud, admiring the beautiful weather and city.
